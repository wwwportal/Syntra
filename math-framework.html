2. Root Space (Distributional Vectors)

Every Arabic root receives a 127-dimensional distributional fingerprint built from its grammatical usage across the entire Quran.

The profile is a normalized probability distribution: for each root, count how often it appears in each feature slot, then normalize so the entries sum to 1.

2.2. Distributional Weight

Measures the structural importance of a root:

$$w = 0.6\;\frac{|\text{surahs}|}{114} + 0.4\;\frac{|\text{morpheme types}|}{|\text{instances}|} \tag{1}$$

where the first term is surah spread and the second is form diversity.

2.3. Instance Anomaly

Scores how unusual a specific usage of a root is (0 = typical, 1 = never-before-seen form):

$$a_i = 1 - p[f_i] \tag{2}$$

where $p$ is the root's profile distribution and $f_i$ is the feature index of the instance. A root that usually appears as a noun but occurs as a verb scores high anomaly on that instance.

2.4. Concordance Distance

The distance between two roots $A$ and $B$ is the Jensen–Shannon divergence of their profiles:

$$D_{\text{JS}}(A \| B) = \frac{1}{2}\,D_{\text{KL}}\!\left(p_A \,\Big\|\, \frac{p_A + p_B}{2}\right) + \frac{1}{2}\,D_{\text{KL}}\!\left(p_B \,\Big\|\, \frac{p_A + p_B}{2}\right) \tag{3}$$

This measures how differently two roots behave grammatically.

3.2. Emphasis Profile

The four per-word signals aggregate into a verse-level emphasis breakdown:

  • Root emphasis — dominated by high-weight words (structurally important roots)
  • Morpheme emphasis — dominated by high-anomaly words (unusual grammatical forms)
  • Position emphasis — dominated by high-drift words (meaning shifts at specific positions)
  • Boundary signal — inverse of minimum coherence (low coherence ⇒ boundary)

The dominant emphasis type tells you what kind of meaning the verse carries: structural (root), exceptional (morpheme), positional (drift), or transitional (boundary).

3.3. Verse Coherence (Phase-Lock)

Measures whether two verses “belong together”—a value in $[0,1]$:

$$\Phi = \frac{1}{3}\bigl(\text{instance\_agreement} + \text{root\_drift} + \text{topic\_overlap}\bigr) \tag{4}$$
  • Instance agreement: average cosine similarity of shared roots' instance profiles
  • Root drift: shift in the distributional landscape between verses
  • Topic overlap: Jaccard overlap of active roots

Thresholds: $\Phi > 0.5$ = continuation, $0.25 < \Phi < 0.5$ = transition, $\Phi < 0.25$ = hard boundary.

4. su(2) Gauge Field

The abjadic gauge field maps Quranic morphological features onto the $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ Lie algebra—the same algebra that describes spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ particles in physics. The mathematical structure (non-commutative gauge connections, holonomy, curvature) is used literally, not as metaphor.

4.1. Pauli Basis

The $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ generators are the Pauli matrices:

$$\sigma_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix},\quad \sigma_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix},\quad \sigma_3 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \tag{5}$$

4.2. Feature-to-su(2) Mapping

Surprisal is the information-theoretic content of a feature:

$$s(f) = \log\!\left(\frac{N}{n_f}\right) \tag{7}$$

where $N = 128{,}000$ (corpus scale) and $n_f$ is the feature's frequency. Rare features contribute more signal. The resulting vector $(x,y,z)$ is normalized to the unit sphere before constructing $H$.

4.3. Curvature Tensor

For two connections $H_\mu$ and $H_\nu$, the curvature is the Lie bracket:

$$R_{\mu\nu} = [H_\mu,\, H_\nu] = H_\mu H_\nu - H_\nu H_\mu \tag{8}$$

Non-zero curvature means the order of traversal matters—the gauge field has genuine structure. The scalar field tension is:

$$\mathcal{T} = \operatorname{Tr}(R_{\mu\nu}^\dagger\, R_{\mu\nu}) \tag{9}$$

4.4. Discrete Holonomy

For a sequence of connections $(H_1, H_2, \ldots, H_n)$—e.g. all instances of a root—compute the path-ordered product:

$$U = H_n \cdot H_{n-1} \cdots H_1 \tag{10}$$

The holonomy angle is extracted via:

$$\kappa = 2\,\arccos\!\left(\left|\frac{\operatorname{Tr}(U)}{2}\right|\right) \tag{11}$$

This is the total “rotation” accumulated by traversing all instances of a root.

4.5. Root Resonance

The holonomy angle $\kappa$ applied across all instances of a root characterizes its semantic behaviour:

  • $\kappa \to 0$: meaning is conserved (consistent usage everywhere)
  • $\kappa \to \pi$: meaning evolves (highly context-dependent)

Validated examples:

  • كتب (k-t-b): $\kappa = 2.35$ — context-dependent
  • ربب (r-b-b): $\kappa = 1.06$ — moderately consistent

4.6. Lyapunov Deviation

Measures how far a specific instance deviates from the root's average gauge state:

$$L = \left\|H - H^*\right\|_F^2 = \operatorname{Tr}\!\bigl((H - H^*)^\dagger (H - H^*)\bigr) \tag{12}$$

where $H^* = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n H_i$ is the mean connection matrix. High-deviation instances are potential falsifiers—places where a proposed meaning for the root might not hold.

5. UOR Ring Substrate

The Universal Object Reference (UOR) provides content-addressed identity for every textual unit. All arithmetic happens in the ring $\mathbb{Z}/2^{256}\mathbb{Z}$.

5.1. Content Addressing

Every atom, morpheme, word, and verse receives a SHA-256 hash as its address. Addressing is compositional and bottom-up:

$$\text{atoms} \;\longrightarrow\; \text{morphemes} \;\longrightarrow\; \text{words} \;\longrightarrow\; \text{verses}$$

A word's address is determined entirely by its constituent atoms, making identity intrinsic to content.

5.2. Dihedral Group Structure

The ring carries a dihedral group $D_{2^{256}}$ generated by two involutions:

$$\operatorname{bnot}(a) = 2^{256} - 1 - a \qquad\text{(bitwise NOT, reflection)}$$
$$\operatorname{neg}(a) = 2^{256} - a \qquad\text{(arithmetic negation, rotation)}$$

Every address belongs to a 4-element orbit: $\{a,\;\operatorname{bnot}(a),\;\operatorname{neg}(a),\;\operatorname{bnot}(\operatorname{neg}(a))\}$.

6. Integration

The three frameworks serve different analytical purposes:

  1. Root Space answers: What does this root do across the Quran? Is this usage normal or exceptional?
  2. su(2) Gauge Field answers: Is this root's meaning conserved or evolving? Where are the tension points?
  3. UOR answers: What is the intrinsic identity of this textual unit? How do units relate compositionally?

A typical analysis flow:

  1. Build root vectors for a passage (root space)
  2. Compute per-word dynamics: weight, anomaly, drift, coherence (verse dynamics)
  3. Detect boundaries and emphasis patterns (verse dynamics)
  4. For roots of interest, compute holonomic resonance (gauge field)
  5. Identify high-deviation instances as falsification targets (Lyapunov)
  6. Track compositional identity through the text hierarchy (UOR)

7.2 Topological Failure Modes: The Structural Blind Spots

The limitations of the current frameworks can be consolidated into three distinct failure modes that define the boundaries of our computational reach.

7.2.1 The "Zero-Edge" Problem (Syntactic Connectivity)

The most significant blind spot is that the system treats the text as a Manifold of Points rather than a Network of Edges. Morphological features (case, gender, number) are stored, but their relational functions are invisible.

  • Government (عَمَل): Prepositions are currently "transparent" particles. The system sees the root ذهب but cannot mathematically distinguish between "Went toward" (ذهب إلى) and "Took away" (ذهب بـ).
  • Syntactic Role: Case endings are captured as flat features, but the predicate-argument structure is not. The system cannot distinguish "X did Y" from "Y did X" if the roots and features are identical.

7.2.2 The "Zero-Signal" Problem (Informational Absence)

Mathematical analysis relies on the presence of signals. However, Quranic eloquence often relies on Omission (حذف) and Metaphor—meaning derived from what is not in the feature vector.

  • Eloquent Silence: In verses like 15:28→29, the text skips from command to result. The math sees the jump (drift) but cannot recover the elided sequence.
  • Figurative Shifts: Root Space averages literal and figurative uses into a single vector. Without a "Domain Violation" model, the math cannot flag when a root like نور (light) shifts from physical to spiritual domains.

7.2.3 The "Infinite-Scale" Problem (Discourse Architecture)

Verse dynamics are limited to local windows. Large-scale structures like Ring Composition or Cross-Surah Parallels remain structurally invisible to the current sequence-based analysis. Perspective-tracing (NCU) remains a human-only operation because the math lacks coreference resolution to track character "trajectories" across verses.

Topological Limit: The current system has high holonomy variance but low connectivity resolution. We know how much a word's meaning rotates, but we don't know who is speaking to whom.
