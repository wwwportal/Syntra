Text Substitution as a Design Pattern for Scope Control and Cognitive Load Optimization

In line with the cognitive load section in page about write-read juxtaposition:

There's this bar at the bottom with different buttons that represent different types of substitutions you can perform, maybe you want to see the details of a section of the text that are relevant to syntax, or morphology, etc.

So, what if you didn't have to open any other panel or page at all. What if all we did is add and subtract context, and provided tools for doing this (the buttons means for different kinds of substitution.)

The set of actions would be:

  • Click on a button to select the type of substitution
  • Left click on a portion of the text to use substitutive expansion and right click for contraction
  • Maybe add another dimension of navigability by using the mouse scroll. Not in a way that would actually scroll up and down but maybe some other action that has to do with going up or down the layers of substitution.

Part of the vision was the mouse icon itself changing color or style or whatever. Such a minimal change that could easily help the user keep track of the currently focused scope/context and the actions they are can be perform at this level.
